KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. It is the only extant representative of the family Phascolarctidae, which is part of the infraclass Metatheria, a group that includes all marsupials. The koala is found in coastal regions of eastern and southern Australia, inhabiting eucalypt forests and woodlands. It is a nocturnal animal, spending most of its life sleeping in trees and feeding on eucalyptus leaves.

The koala's diet consists almost exclusively of eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most animals. They have a specialized digestive system that allows them to detoxify the chemicals in the leaves. Koalas are known for their distinctive appearance, with large round ears, a flat nose, and a stout body covered in thick fur. They are also known for their unique vocalizations, which include grunts, snores, and bellows.
